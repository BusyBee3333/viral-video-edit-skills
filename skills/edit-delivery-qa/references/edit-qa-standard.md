# Edit QA Standard

## Ready Criteria

An edit is ready when:

- specs match the requested format
- audio is present and trimmed/faded intentionally
- contact sheet has active, readable, intentional frames
- timeline density fits the song energy
- source mix supports the creative brief
- accent footage punctuates the edit rather than taking over
- no generated placeholders remain when real footage was required

## Conteto Machino Lessons

- Contact sheets are mandatory for fast montage work.
- Real laser/festival footage should replace generated laser placeholders for final cuts.
- Crowd-only clips can be excellent if they are explosive and placed after impact moments.
- Vertical gameplay needs a readable foreground strip; full-frame vertical crops often lose the important action.
- Timeline CSVs and brief JSON files make iterative critique much easier because they expose segment count, source mix, and intent.

## Suggested Final Response Shape

Keep final QA reports short:

- state pass/fail/readiness
- list the most important specs
- name any visible issues
- mention generated outputs such as contact sheet or QA report
- recommend the next revision only when needed
