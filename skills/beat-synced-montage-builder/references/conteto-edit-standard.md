# Conteto Machino Edit Standard

Use this reference when adapting the Smash Mash / Conteto Machino workflow to another montage.

## Baseline Target

- Duration: `30-40s` for proof/social cuts.
- Frame rate: `30fps` minimum.
- Landscape target: `1920x1080`.
- Vertical target: `1080x1920`.
- Audio: clean music excerpt, no accidental source audio unless intentionally mixed.
- Cut density: about `70-90` cuts for a `35s` high-energy montage.
- Average segment length: about `0.35-0.50s`.
- Deliverables: final video, timeline CSV, contact sheet, brief JSON.

## Why The Standard Worked

The best reference edit felt timed to the music because footage changed almost every beat, source windows were motion-scored, and priority musical hits got the strongest visual moments.

The mix stayed action-first but used human and light accents as punctuation:

- gameplay/action as the backbone
- real crowd or player reactions after big hits
- real lasers, strobes, beams, smoke, or festival lights on drops/fills
- occasional source-context clips, such as character select or menu moments, only when deliberate

## Footage Rules

Prefer:

- obvious impacts
- KO zooms, spikes, meteor smashes, zero-to-deaths, fast combo strings
- fast camera motion or visual reveals
- explosive crowd and player popoffs
- real festival/concert lasers and stage-light sweeps

Use selectively:

- nature or whimsical inserts
- character/menu/source-context clips
- abstract light bursts

Reject or heavily deprioritize:

- title cards
- interviews
- blank stages or menus without a deliberate role
- overcropped frames that lose action direction
- low-motion setup during peak sections

## QA Expectations

Before delivery, verify:

- actual duration, resolution, frame rate, and audio stream
- contact sheet shows mostly active/intentional frames
- timeline CSV has healthy cut density and source variety
- priority-5 markers are served by strong footage
- accents support gameplay/action instead of replacing too much of it
